Year 1 · 2025 · Delivered

Laying the groundwork

Account Dashboard

A unified front door giving members a single location for account, tools, licences and platform status. Previously scattered across three separate systems.

Platform Stability

The production infrastructure was showing strain under a growing user base. Targeted profiling, caching and infrastructure hardening delivered the reliable baseline Year 2 needed.

Year 3 · 2027 · The bigger initiatives unlock

The architectural choices compound

With a stable platform and integrated tooling underneath, initiatives that would not have been feasible — or responsible — to attempt earlier become deliverable.

Integrated Mapping Tool

Mapping SNOMED CT to ICD, LOINC, OPCS and other classifications becomes a first-class capability with purpose-built tooling. Better tooling means more consistent, higher-quality maps — with direct implications for clinical interoperability across healthcare systems globally.

Content Request Redesign

The mechanism through which members and community contributors request changes to SNOMED CT is slower and more manual than it needs to be. A redesigned CRS with AI-assisted submission support compresses the feedback loop between community and terminology.

Extension Marketplace

A platform where SNOMED CT extensions can be shared, discovered and validated by the community. The ambition: an ecosystem that grows in ways SNOMED International cannot anticipate or resource alone — contributors building on each other's work across the network.

The AI Story

SNOMED CT is not a passive reference for AI systems to consume.

It is an active component of safe, interoperable clinical AI — a structuring layer, a quality backbone, a guardrail.

The AI strategy is designed to demonstrate that across multiple fronts simultaneously — translation, benchmarking, content quality, and platform integration. Taken together, these are not pilot projects hedging against uncertainty. They are a coordinated portfolio positioning SNOMED CT at the centre of the clinical AI landscape.

AI Workstream · Content Quality

Quality improvement at the point of origin, not retrospectively

Rather than attempting to audit the 366,000+ concepts in SNOMED CT after the fact, the focus has shifted to where quality problems originate: the content request process.

When a member or community contributor submits a change request, the quality of their clinical reasoning and evidence directly determines how quickly it can be reviewed. AI integration into that process — guiding better submissions, surfacing relevant references, flagging evidence gaps before a request arrives — improves quality at source.

An honest note: AI-assisted quality improvement applied retrospectively at scale was tested in 2025 and found to be disappointing — individual runs improved 300–400 concepts, but with 366,000+ remaining, the approach did not scale. The upstream approach is more tractable, and more honest about where AI can genuinely contribute.
